Agencies

(DICT) Department of Information and Communication Technology

Established to be the primary policy, planning, coordinating, implementing, and administrative entity for ICT development, the DICT aims to ensure the provision of a reliable and cost-efficient communication infrastructure and services.

General Statistics

Total Cybercrime Cases (Year-to-Date): In 2024, the DICT processed approximately 2,500 cybersecurity cases, with 60 attributed to state-level hackers employing advanced techniques.Number of Active ICT Projects: Specific figures on active ICT projects are not readily available. However, the DICT has been involved in numerous initiatives aimed at improving the country's digital infrastructure and services. Free Wi-Fi Access Points Installed: • As of November 2024, over 13,000 free Wi-Fi sites had been established nationwide, with 16,251 access points installed. Status Indicators: • The DICT continually monitors and updates its systems to address cybersecurity threats, processing millions of cyber threats daily to safeguard government and public networks.

General Information

The Logo uses curve radiating lines to represent the pulsating radio waves or frequencies, with the lines using the tri-colors of the National Flag: red, white, and blue. At the center of the sphere is the yellow silhouette of the map of the Philippines, in lieu of the yellow sun and stars of the Philippine Flag. The curve lines outside the map also indicate the sometimes placid or surging waters of the Philippine archipelago, evoking the feeling of dynamism brought about by ICT. The 17 connected dots within the silhouette of the map represent the 17 Philippine/DICT regions connected by technology. The numbers around the spherical Logo are the binary equivalent of the acronym DICT and 2016, the year the Department was established under Republic Act (R.A.) No. 10844. The straight lines radiating from the spherical Logo and intersecting the acronym DICT (outside the sphere) represent the frequency waves as they travel through space in straight lines; they also represent the encompassing reach of ICT and telecommunications. The colored sphere is not circumscribed because communications, the service, are without borders.

List of Responsibilities

Develop and implement national ICT policies and guidelines. Promote digital literacy and ICT education. Enhance cybersecurity measures and protect critical ICT infrastructures. Provide equitable access to ICT services across the country. Foster the growth of ICT-enabled industries.

(DOJ) Department Of Justice

The Department of Justice – Office of Cybercrime (DOJ-OOC) is the lead agency in the Philippines for enforcing the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 (RA 10175). It is responsible for handling the investigation, prosecution, and coordination of cybercrime-related cases such as hacking, identity theft, online scams, and cybersex trafficking. The DOJ-OOC also works closely with both local and international partners to strengthen cybersecurity measures, conduct training, and raise public awareness. Its official website serves as a central hub for cybercrime reporting, public advisories, legal updates, and information on the government's digital security initiatives.

General Statistics

Total Cybercrime Cases (e.g., YTD): Specific year-to-date statistics on total cybercrime cases handled by the DOJ are not readily available in the provided sources. However, during the enhanced community quarantine in 2020, reports of online child sexual exploitation nearly tripled, indicating a significant rise in cybercrime incidents during that period.

General Information

The DOJ's seal features three stars representing the Philippines' main geographical regions. A balanced scale over a sword symbolizes justice and authority, set against a map of the Philippines. The Latin phrase "Justitiae Pax Opus" translates to "Justice for All."

Investigating crimes and prosecuting offenders. Providing legal counsel to government agencies Implementing laws related to immigration and citizenship. (NBI) Established on November 13, 1936, the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) is a premier investigative agency under the Department of Justice (DOJ) in the Philippines. Patterned after the United States' FBI, the NBI is tasked with investigating and combating various crimes, including cybercrime, human trafficking, organized crime, corruption, and terrorism. It also provides technical forensic services and intelligence gathering to support the Philippine criminal justice system. The NBI's mission is to uphold justice through impartial, efficient, and accountable investigative services.

Total Cybercrime Cases (Year-to-Date):
Specific year-to-date statistics for cybercrime cases handled by the NBI are not publicly detailed. However,
the Philippine National Police (PNP) reported a 36% decrease in cybercrime cases in the first half of 2024,
with 8,177 complaints from January to June, down from 12,808 in the same period in 2023.

The NBI's seal features a shield symbolizing protection, with the Philippine sun and three stars representing the nation. A balanced scale signifies justice, and the sword denotes authority and enforcement.

Investigate major high-profile cases and offenses against Philippine laws.
Conduct forensic examinations and provide technical services.
Collect and analyze intelligence related to criminal activities.
Monitor and ensure compliance with international data protection standards.
Collaborate with local and international law enforcement agencies.

(NPC) The NPC serves as the country's privacy watchdog, tasked with enforcing the Data Privacy Act of 2012 and ensuring the Philippines' compliance with international data protection standards.

As of August 31, 2024, the NPC reported a total of 6,847,611,386 security incidents from 2018 to 2023.
Number of Active ICT Projects: The NPC is actively involved in various projects, including the Data Privacy Competency Program aimed at enhancing data privacy education in the Philippines.
Free Wi-Fi Access Points Installed: The installation of free Wi-Fi access points is managed by the Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT), not the NPC.
Status Indicators: The NPC continually monitors and updates its systems to address data privacy concerns and ensure compliance with regulations.

A pearl buried inside a tightly-shut shell is practically worthless. Government information is a pearl, meant to be shared with the public in order to maximize its inherent value.
The Transparency Seal, depicted by a pearl shining out of an open shell, is a symbol of a policy shift towards openness in access to government information.
This initiative is envisioned as a step in the right direction towards solidifying the position of the Philippines as the Pearl of the Orient – a shining example for democratic virtue in the region.
